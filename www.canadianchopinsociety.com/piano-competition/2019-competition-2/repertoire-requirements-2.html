Fifth Canadian Chopin Piano Competition
Required Repertoire

(all works by Chopin)

Junior Division

Any TWO etudes by Chopin
Any ONE nocturne by Chopin
Any ONE additional work by Chopin

Time Limit – 22 minutes

Senior Division

Stage One – Preliminary Round

 i. Two etudes, one from each of the groups below:

a) C major, op. 10 no. 1
C sharp minor, op. 10 no. 4
G flat major, op. 10 no. 5
F major, op. 10 no. 8
C minor, op. 10 no. 12
A minor, op. 25 no. 11
b) A minor, op.10 no.2
C major, op. 10 no. 7
A flat major, op. 10 no. 10
E flat major, op. 10 no. 11
A minor, op. 25 no. 4
E minor, op. 25 no. 5
G sharp minor, op. 25 no. 6
B minor, op. 25 no. 10
 ii. One of the following works:
Nocturne in B major, op. 9 no. 3
Nocturne in C sharp minor, op. 27 no. 1
Nocturne in D flat major, op. 27 no. 2
Nocturne in G major, op. 37 no. 2
Nocturne in C minor, op. 48 no. 1
Nocturne in F sharp minor, op. 48 no. 2
Nocturne in E flat major, op. 55 no. 2
Nocturne in B major, op. 62 no. 1
Nocturne in E major, op 62 no. 2
Etude in E major, op. 10 no. 3
Etude in E flat minor op. 10 no. 6
Etude in C sharp minor, op. 25 no. 7
 iii. One of the following works:
Ballade in G minor, op. 23
Ballade in F major, op. 38
Ballade in A flat major, op. 47
Ballade in F minor, op. 52
Barcarolle in F sharp major, op. 60
Fantasy in F minor, op. 49
iv. One of the following works:
Andante Spianato and Polonaise in E flat major, op. 22
Polonaise in F sharp minor, op. 44
Polonaise in A flat major, op. 53
Polonaises op. 26 no. 1 and no.2

The works may be performed in any order, with the exception of the two compulsory etudes, which must be played one after the other.

Stage Two – Semi-Finals

i. One of the following full sets of Mazurkas:
Opus 17
Opus 24
Opus 30
Opus 33
Opus 41
Opus 50
Opus 56
Opus 59
ii. One of the following works:
Sonata in B flat minor, op. 35
Sonata in B minor, op. 58
All Preludes,  op. 28

Stage Three – Finals

One of the following concertos , to be performed with String Quartert:
Concerto in E minor op. 11
Concerto in F Minor Op. 21
